Title: TSMC shares fall in premarket despite record-breaking Q2 profits
URL: https://cryptobriefing.com/tsmc-shares-fall-premarket-record-profits/
Time Published: 2026-07-16T09:47:22Z
Full Content:
Searching... The world's most important chipmaker posted its best quarter ever, and investors shrugged. Share Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company reported second-quarter results on July 16 that were, by any reasonable measure, extraordinary. Revenue hit $40.2 billion, up 36% from the same period a year ago. Net profit came in at roughly $21.98 billion. And yet, TSM shares slipped about 0.2% in premarket trading. TSMC’s gross margin landed at 67.7%. Its operating margin reached 60.3%. The net profit figure of T$706.56 billion also cleared Bloomberg’s consensus estimate of T$623.73 billion by a meaningful margin. The company also issued forward guidance that would make most chipmakers blush. TSMC expects Q3 2026 revenue to land between $44.6 billion and $45.8 billion. TSMC manufactures the most advanced chips in the world, and it does so primarily from Taiwan. Any shift in the political temperature between Beijing and Taipei, or between Washington and either of them, gets priced into TSM shares with remarkable speed. This is not a new tension. TSMC has been navigating it for years, which is partly why the company has been expanding manufacturing capacity in Arizona and Japan. The 36% revenue growth and 77% profit surge reflect a fundamental shift in what the world’s largest technology companies need. Hyperscalers, meaning the Microsofts, Googles, and Amazons of the world, are spending to build AI infrastructure. TSMC makes the chips that go into the GPUs and custom accelerators that make that infrastructure run. The GPU shortage that has affected crypto mining economics and AI compute costs alike flows directly from the same wafer capacity dynamics that TSMC controls. When AI demand keeps TSMC’s fabs running at full tilt on the most advanced process nodes, that shapes pricing and availability across the broader chip ecosystem. TSMC’s Q3 guidance of $44.6 billion to $45.8 billion implies continued acceleration. If the company hits the midpoint of that range, it will have grown revenue by roughly a third in a single year. The risk case is not about demand. The risk case is about the discount rate applied to a company whose primary manufacturing base sits in one of the world’s most geopolitically sensitive locations.
