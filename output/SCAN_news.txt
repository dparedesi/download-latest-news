Mechanism: Intel captures value by selling excess fab capacity to external fabless customers (e.g., Google, Nvidia). Mechanism: The thesis breaks if the foundry (IFS) fails to become profitable, remaining a multi-billion-dollar quarterly drag on earnings, while the core data center business continues to cede its most profitable market share to competitors. Even a modest slip in 18A timing or a single quarter of accelerated server share loss could trigger a sharp multiple compression. Supporting Evidence: Diversification Beats Conviction Intel’s turnaround is a compelling story, but it hinges on a few key outcomes. 18A yields, foundry customer conversion, and server share stabilization.
