Behind the celebratory noise lies an uncomfortable truth. The acceleration of symbolic milestones does not reflect an acceleration of growth, but a simple mathematical reality. From as far back as 1896 until the day the Dow Jones reached 10,000 points, it took 28,000 trading sessions—no less than 82 years. For the next 10,000 points, it took just 4,500 trading sessions. The jump from 40,000 to 50,000 points corresponds to a 25% gain. The exact same percentage is needed for the Dow to reach 60,000 points. But to achieve the “target” of 100,000 points requires a doubling from current levels.
