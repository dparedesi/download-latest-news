That methodology change is what produced the 22% to 28% upward revision to out-year estimates for Broadcom. Broadcom designs custom AI accelerators, AI Ethernet networking silicon, and broader semiconductor solutions, paired with the VMware software franchise. The bull case for Broadcom stock rests on continued hyperscaler AI silicon demand and VMware execution at 68% adjusted EBITDA margins. The bear case centers on customer concentration risk and competitive pressure from NVIDIA (NASDAQ:NVDA) in adjacent AI compute markets. For prudent investors, the Wells Fargo revision is a signal that the AI infrastructure cycle may still be in earlier innings than consensus models suggest. Given Broadcom’s scale, however, any moderation in hyperscaler capex could be felt immediately, so position sizing matters more than conviction here.
