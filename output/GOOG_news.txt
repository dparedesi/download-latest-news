Copyright 2025 - All Right Reserved. Sunday Guardian Live By: Amreen AhmadLast Updated: June 22, 2026 21:45:25 IST Alphabet Inc Class C Stock Down Today: The class C stocks of Alphabet Inc. (GOOG) were subjected to heavy selling activity on June 22 as they fell by 3.77% while GOOG underperformed relative to the overall performance of the Software & IT Services sector, where it fell 0.25%, as well as compared to many of its other large-cap peers in the tech industry. This drop made Alphabet move farther away from its record high of $408.61 hit on May 18. The move surprised many investors because Alphabet’s underlying business continues to post strong revenue and cloud growth however, Wall Street is increasingly focused on the costs and risks associated with the company’s ambitious artificial intelligence expansion. The main reason for the decline on Monday is the growing worry regarding the large amount of money that Alphabet is allocating towards artificial intelligence. There is doubt regarding the company’s ability to remain profitable with such an unprecedented amount of allocation to artificial intelligence. In mid-April, Alphabet revealed its intention of raising about $84.75 billion via an equity offering to finance its growing aspirations in the field of AI. It caused concerns related to the potential dilution of shareholders and represented an end to the reputation of the cash-filled tech firm that engaged in vigorous share repurchase programs while in 2026, Alphabet is expected to make capital expenditures ranging from $180 billion to $190 billion. One of the metrics catching a lot of attention at the moment is free cash flow and even though Alphabet recorded revenues of $109.9 billion in the first quarter of 2026, free cash flow dropped by 47% from the previous year to reach $10.1 billion due to increased expenditure on AI technology. Issues faced by investors got worse as some of the top-notch AI executives in the firm quit their posts. It seems like some of the leading individuals linked to Gemini and Google DeepMind have switched to other firms like OpenAI and Anthropic. Even though Alphabet still has plenty of AI researchers on its rolls, the exodus has raised questions about whether the firm is falling behind. Legal challenges are increasing for Alphabet Inc., which faces investigations into its search engine and advertising services by authorities in the US, Europe and the UK. A number of antitrust cases have the potential to force the company to change the way it operates and possibly lead to fines while this constitutes one of the biggest risks for the stock. Though there has been a selloff, Alphabet is performing exceptionally well operationally. The first quarter saw a rise of 22% in revenues YoY to $109.9 billion, with Google Cloud revenues shooting up 63% to $20 billion. Operating income increased by 30% to $39.7 billion and operating margins improved to 36.1% while the cloud backlog stood at an astounding $460 billion. Technically speaking, there is some sort of contradiction between these two indicators while the stock MACD of -1.745 indicates that there is some bearish momentum in the stock. NASDAQ: GOOG, Alphabet Inc Class C shares, fell 3.77% on June 22 as investors reacted to shifting expectations around Google’s AI strategy and broader tech market pressure while the decline reflects short-term sentiment changes, though long-term outlook remains tied to AI innovation, advertising strength and cloud growth performance. Although Wall Street faces some near-term challenges, its outlook is mostly bullish while the analysts still expect an average price target of around $417.14, with some projections as high as $475. Brokers also still recommend Alphabet as a Buy, attributing their positive stance to robust cloud business, AI investments and healthy advertising revenues however AI investments, talent retention and regulations will continue drawing investor attention through the rest of 2026. The current fall in Alphabet seems to be less about deteriorating fundamentals and more about the uncertainty around the firm’s AI revolution. Alphabet continues to operate one of the most profitable firms globally, recording an annual revenue of $402.84 billion and net income of $132.17 billion. At present, the Street is trying to figure out if the massive spending on AI at Alphabet would end up being just a phase or form the base of its future growth for a decade to come.
