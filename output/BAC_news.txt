And while Wednesday's Fed meeting did little to dent investors' outlook, a small tick up in inflation could mean trouble ahead, Doll said. "What I am concerned about with the inflation rate is if it rounds to 3% instead of 2% this year," he said. "The market is, in my view, discounting a decline in the inflation rate, and if it doesn't happen, and it stays closer to 3, could there be a risk of a few points in PE [ratios], which would be painful." To stay insulated from these risks, Doll said the top quality he's looking for in stocks today is high-free cash flow levels, as measured by return on equity. Focusing on this metric means owning efficient companies that generate strong profits. "If I can have above average return on equity and below average price of free cash flow, I think I have a recipe to do OK in a crazy environment," Doll said. The market sector that's most ripe with high-ROE stocks picks is financials, Doll said. He said there are four stocks in particular that fit the bill and that he's betting on: Bank of America (BAC), JPMorgan (JPM), Wells Fargo (WFC), and Citi (C). "The big banks are prototypical examples," he said. "Among the banks, they have, the best balance sheets," he continued, adding that "they tend to be cheaper than the other parts of the financial sector." All four of the stocks are negative so far in 2026. Shares of Citi have vastly outperformed the other three over the last 12 months, returning 42%. The State Street Financial Select Sector SPDR ETF (XLF) has underperformed the S&P 500 in the last year, gaining just 4%.
