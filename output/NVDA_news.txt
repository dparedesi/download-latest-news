Corning stock is the bigger winner, jumping 14% to $185. The deal landed before noon on the same day Corning is hosting its investor event, where management plans to unveil a new Photonics Market-Access Platform. The framing matters. Optical connectivity is increasingly the choke point for AI scaling, and NVIDIA just put real capital behind solving it. For more on NVIDIA’s recent strategic moves, see our NVIDIA AI infrastructure outlook. According to the regulatory filing, NVIDIA will purchase 3 million Corning shares at $0.0001 each and receive warrants to buy up to 15 million additional shares at $180 apiece. That structure ties NVIDIA’s upside directly to Corning’s execution on AI capacity. Corning will use the partnership to build three new U.S. factories in North Carolina and Texas, expanding optical connectivity manufacturing capacity by tenfold and fiber production capacity by 50%. The buildout is expected to add over 3,000 American jobs. Corning’s Q1 2026 report, released April 28, already flagged Optical Communications revenue of $1.85 billion, up 36% year over year. Today’s deal extends that growth runway and signals NVIDIA’s confidence in sustained AI demand visibility. Inside an AI data center, copper handles short-distance links cheaply, but it loses the bandwidth-distance fight at hyperscale. AI training and inference workloads require massive parallel data movement between GPUs, racks, and clusters, often spanning longer distances than copper can efficiently serve. NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang has framed AI compute as growing exponentially, with demand for both copper and optic capacity outpacing supply. As GPU clusters scale to hundreds of thousands of accelerators, the optical layer is the difference between a system that runs at full utilization and one that bottlenecks on data movement. Corning’s fiber and silicon photonics products plug directly into NVIDIA’s Spectrum-X and Quantum-X networking roadmap. The 10x capacity expansion suggests NVIDIA expects optical demand to scale with each Blackwell, Blackwell Ultra, and Rubin generation. Corning is quickly becoming a preferred AI infrastructure supplier. In January, the company struck a multiyear deal worth up to $6 billion with Meta Platforms (NASDAQ:META), and Q1 2026 commentary flagged two additional hyperscale customers signing similar long-term deals. The valuation has already moved sharply ahead of fundamentals.
