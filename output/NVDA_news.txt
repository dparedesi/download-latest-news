Big firms have strict rules around trading off research, placing stocks on so-called restricted lists. One question is: Do the prosecutors who went after Left understand the law or just want to make short selling de facto illegal? Another: Did they know average people on the jury would buy a weird case because they don’t know much about what the shorts do? In my career covering finance, I’ve seen some shady short-selling tips, but the vast majority of market upheavals, like the dot-com crash of 2000 or the 2008-2009 financial crisis, have been on the long side — people buying stuff based on irrational exuberance and touts.
