Title: 'Focus on revenue realism': How to play the space-stock boom, and 6 stocks to buy now, according to Stifel
URL: https://www.businessinsider.com/top-space-stocks-to-buy-spacex-rocket-lab-kratos-stifel-2026-7#article
Time Published: 2026-07-09T09:45:01Z
Full Content:
Investing in space might feel like throwing money into a black hole, but Stifel says there are some key considerations to help navigate the trade. Since the blockbuster SpaceX IPO that captivated markets in June, the broader space trade has come into focus as investors try to make sense of technology's next frontier. Stifel's Jonathan Siegmann shared some advice on how to investing in the theme in a presentation published on July 6. "Space stocks were mostly ignorable as an industry group until late 2024," Siegmann wrote, adding that the upswing in 2025 and 2026 has been "unprecedented in scope and duration." As for how to invest, "Focus on revenue realism." "We believe spaces stocks with the highest revenue estimate revisions most often outperformed those stocks with lowest revisions." Other tips and things to look for include: The analysis notes that "space is hard," but failures often lead to buying opportunities. In the aftermath of space catastrophes like launch failures or explosions, shares of many firms that have already scaled recover, while the already struggling players do not. Here are the six space stocks that Stifel has a "Buy" rating on. Implied upside: 30%Stock joined the Nasdaq 100 this week, though shares have continued to struggle since the June 11 IPO, tumbling below $150 this week, the level where trading began on June 12. Yet, Stifle, like most of Wall Street, is bullish, noting SpaceX's transition from a leader in rocket launches to a multi-faceted aerospace company. "The company operates three synergistic segments: Launch Services (the logistics backbone), Starlink (the global connectivity platform), and AI (compute infrastructure and frontier AI models) SpaceX currently commands over 60% of the global commercial launch market as of 2025," Siegmann wrote. The space tech company is one of only two Canadian firms selected by the US Missile Defense Agency to be part of SHIELD IDIQ, though MDA Space also offers exposure to several high growth corners of the tech sector. It also has ample dry powder for M&A following its US listing in March. Stifel says this should allow MDA Space to establish a local manufacturing presence in the US, mitigating the impact of tariffs and giving it access to defense contracts limited to companies with US production capabilities. Manufacturing firm Applied Aerospace builds hardware for space exploration. It has worked with numerous industry incumbents, providing propellant tanks and landing system tubes for the Blue Origin New Glenn and landing legs for the SpaceX Falcon 9. Stifel described it as "Entrenched in structurally growing defense & space end-markets: $1B+ backlog underpins ~14% organic revenue CAGR through 2028 driven by missile replenishment, air-dominance investment, and commercial-space proliferation." This under-the-radar space stock is up 117% year-to-date and Stifel expects its rally to contine. Spire Global provides data and analytics for space companies. Stifel says it expects Spire Global to benefit from civil procurement, with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration expected to purchase billions in weather data in the coming decade. It also sees growth driven by US and international defense contracts and a partnership with Deloitte for cyber defense and a manufacturing agreement with Schaeffler to build "sovereign European space infrastructure."
