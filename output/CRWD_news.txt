Title: CrowdStrike partners with Cerebras to enhance AI detection and response
URL: https://cryptobriefing.com/crowdstrike-cerebras-ai-security-partnership/
Time Published: 2026-07-22T14:18:14Z
Full Content:
Searching... The cybersecurity giant will run its Falcon AIDR models on Cerebras hardware, while the chip startup adopts CrowdStrike's platform to protect its own operations. Share CrowdStrike and Cerebras Systems are teaming up to tackle one of the fastest-growing problems in enterprise tech: securing AI infrastructure before attackers figure out how to exploit it. The partnership, announced on July 22, pairs CrowdStrike’s Falcon AI Detection and Response platform with Cerebras’ high-speed inference hardware. The deal works in both directions. CrowdStrike will deploy its Falcon AIDR models on Cerebras’ chips to accelerate threat detection, while Cerebras will standardize on the Falcon platform to shore up its own cybersecurity posture. Cerebras CISO Naor Penso put it simply: “every millisecond matters” in AI security. That’s not hyperbole when you’re talking about protecting prompt layers, AI agents, data pipelines, and identity management systems, all of which are attack surfaces that didn’t meaningfully exist five years ago. CrowdStrike’s Falcon AIDR platform, which reached general availability on December 15, 2025, was built specifically to address these emerging vectors. Running those models on Cerebras’ inference-optimized hardware should meaningfully reduce the latency between detection and response. CrowdStrike CBO Daniel Bernard framed the partnership as combining “the world’s best security AI and the world’s fastest inference.” This deal arrives at a particularly interesting moment for Cerebras. The company closed its IPO on May 15, 2026, raising approximately $6.38 billion. Cerebras trades on NASDAQ under the ticker CBRS, and this CrowdStrike partnership adds another high-profile name to a roster that already includes collaborations with OpenAI and Amazon. For CrowdStrike, the partnership extends its reach deeper into the AI infrastructure stack. The company (NASDAQ: CRWD) has been building out its AI security capabilities, and Falcon AIDR represents its flagship bet on the category. Partnering with a hardware vendor like Cerebras gives CrowdStrike a performance advantage that’s hard to replicate with general-purpose compute. For CrowdStrike shareholders, the Cerebras deal validates Falcon AIDR’s positioning as a platform that hardware vendors want to integrate with. Being the security layer that chip companies standardize on is a fundamentally different value proposition than being one of several endpoint protection vendors on a procurement shortlist. For Cerebras investors, the partnership signals that the company’s hardware has utility beyond training and general inference workloads. Security is a sticky, high-margin use case. Once an enterprise deploys CrowdStrike’s detection models on Cerebras silicon, the switching costs become substantial. AUSTIN, Texas and SUNNYVALE, Calif., July 22, 2026 (GLOBE NEWSWIRE) -- CrowdStrike (NASDAQ: CRWD) and Cerebras Systems (NASDAQ: CBRS) today announced a strategic partnership between the industry’s leading AI-native security platform and the world's fastest AI inference. CrowdStrike will leverage Cerebras’s industry-leading inference speed to help power Falcon AI Detection and Response (AIDR), while Cerebras standardizes on the CrowdStrike Falcon® platform to secure its business. Together, the companies are pairing the inference speed with AI-native security for enterprises building and deploying AI at scale. Cybersecurity is fundamentally changed by AI, as attacks are faster, more targeted, and harder to detect. Defending against AI-accelerated threats requires security that reasons and acts at machine speed. Adversaries move across domains in seconds, and the window to detect and respond has collapsed. Security teams must respond with larger, smarter models that run fast enough for real-time protection, stopping more threats sooner, including those that legacy systems would have missed. “Inference is where AI creates value, and cybersecurity is one of the clearest examples of where speed matters most,” said Naor Penso, CISO, Cerebras. “Security cannot wait in the queue for slow AI while an attack unfolds. Every millisecond matters. It determines whether AI prevents an attack or explains what happened afterward. CrowdStrike built the industry’s leading cybersecurity platform, and Cerebras delivers the world’s fastest inference. Together, we enable real-time AI security to stay ahead of today’s threats.” The partnership extends CrowdStrike's leadership in AIDR, the category it pioneered to secure AI across every surface it operates on, from data and models to agents and identities. It also marks a new frontier for Cerebras, bringing the inference speed trusted by the world's most demanding AI workloads to one of AI’s most consequential applications: securing the enterprise in real-time. Cerebras's own adoption of Falcon reflects the shift: the companies building AI infrastructure are turning to CrowdStrike to protect their AI. “If you want to build AI, you need the fastest compute. If you want to secure AI, you need CrowdStrike. By running Falcon AIDR models on Cerebras, we put the world’s best security AI on the world’s fastest inference. Fast, secure AI drives rapid adoption,” said Daniel Bernard, chief business officer at CrowdStrike. “Cerebras is one of the most important names in AI compute, and they’ve standardized on the Falcon platform to secure their own innovation. The companies building the future of AI are choosing CrowdStrike to protect it.” As AI reshapes the enterprise, the world's innovators build on the fastest AI infrastructure, powered by Cerebras and secured by CrowdStrike.
